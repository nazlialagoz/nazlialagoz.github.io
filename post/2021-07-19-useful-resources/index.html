Book Suggestions

  • Lean Startup - Eric Ries Treat your PhD process like a startup and decrease frustrations

  • Algorithms to Live By - Brian Christian and Tom Griffiths Effective methods to apply when making decisions and trying to achieve something I especially found Chapters 2 (Explore/Exploit: The Latest vs. Greatest) and 7 (Overfitting: When to Think Less) useful, especially for PhD process and your 20s.

  • Thinking in Bets - Annie Duke Along the same lines as the book above.

  • Scrum: The Art of Doing Twice the Work in Half the Time Book by J. J. Sutherland Make your work visible, keep track of things and avoid frustrations

  • Deep Learning for Coders with Fastai and PyTorch - Jeremy Howard and Sylvain Gugger Accessible guide to Machine Learning with Python. They also have a related web lectures here. After you get the basics with this book and Andrew Ng’s coursera course you can check out ML for economics here.

  • The Road from Coorain - Jill Ker Conway

  • Behave - Robert Sapolsky A biological perspective on human behavior and emotions

  • The Selfish Gene - Richard Dawkins
